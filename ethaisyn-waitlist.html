Early Access · Annotation Toolkit

Your clinical judgment
deserves a record.

When an AI tool misreads your patient, that insight disappears. EthAiSyn is building the first cross-platform space where mental health professionals can collectively document AI failures — and build the shared standards the field is missing.

Platforms like Upheal and Eleos let you correct AI outputs within their systems. Those corrections stay siloed. A peer support specialist at one organization cannot see what a clinician at another is noticing. There is no shared record. EthAiSyn is built to create that layer — and it does not yet exist anywhere.

What early access includes

  • Beta access to the annotation toolkit before public launch
  • Direct input into which AI failure patterns we prioritize documenting first
  • Named contributor status in the Year 1 public report shared with NAMI North Texas and DFW partners
  • Community of DFW practitioners building shared interpretive standards together

No spam. No commitment. Just your name on the list. Dallas-Fort Worth practitioners especially welcome.
