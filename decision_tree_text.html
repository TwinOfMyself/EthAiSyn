Decision Tree · Coordination Protocol

Verbal Callback Commitment
Made During a Case Management Call

A step-by-step process guide for what should happen when a Case Manager makes a promise to a patient that cannot be resolved in the same interaction — the coordination gap the current workflow cannot see.

Starting Point
A Case Manager has just ended a contact call in which they made a verbal commitment to follow up on an urgent patient need — medication access, a clinical query, or a referral — that could not be resolved during the call itself. Who knows about this commitment: the CM only. The system has no record of it yet.
Step-by-Step Process
1
Moment of commitment
CM Makes Verbal Callback Commitment During Call
This is the step the current IMH workflow skips entirely. Neither the ES Visit nor Post-Discharge workflow has a step for logging a commitment made inside a completed contact call.
Joint Commission Sentinel Event Alert 58 — every handoff requires a documented action list before responsibility transfers
Immediate required action — before ending the call
→ CM logs the open commitment in the .cmutel entry before closing the contact note
→ Log must include: (1) nature of commitment, (2) what was promised to patient, (3) timeframe given, (4) what resolution requires — e.g. prescribing doctor confirmation, pharmacy check, on-call referral
→ If no timeframe was stated to patient: default to same business day
Is this a Friday call, or does the promised timeframe cross end-of-day or a public holiday?
No — Standard business day, resolution achievable same day
Proceed to Step 2: Same-Day Resolution Attempt
Yes — Friday call, or timeframe crosses EOD or weekend
Commitment cannot survive on one person's memory across a coverage gap →
Proceed to Step 3: Weekend / EOD Handover Protocol
2
Standard resolution path
Same-Day Resolution Attempt
SAMHSA (2019) — post-discharge follow-up within 24 hours is an institutional responsibility, not an individual one
Action
→ CM contacts prescribing doctor / pharmacy / relevant party to resolve the need within the same business day
→ All contact attempts documented in .cmutel with timestamps
Was the commitment resolved within the same business day?
Yes — Resolved
CM calls patient back to confirm resolution
CM updates .cmutel: commitment closed, resolution documented, patient informed
No further escalation required
Owner: CM · Timeframe: Same business day
No — Unresolved by end of business day
CM updates .cmutel: attempted, unresolved, reason documented
CM notifies Team Lead before leaving: open commitment flagged, patient informed of delay, risk level noted
Is there identified clinical risk attached to the unresolved need?
Yes — Risk identified (e.g. medication gap, safety concern)
Escalate to Team Lead and Consultant same day, before close of business
Consultant determines whether on-call coverage or emergency pharmacy access is needed
Team Lead assigns a covering CM to hold the commitment overnight
Patient is called back to communicate delay and receive a specific new timeframe
Owner: Team Lead + Consultant · Timeframe: Before EOD, same day
No — No immediate clinical risk
CM flags in .cmutel: unresolved, low risk, follow-up required next business day
CM sets system reminder (calendar alert or task flag) for first action next morning
Team Lead informed via in-basket before CM leaves
Owner: CM (next morning) · Timeframe: First thing next business day
3
Critical gap node
Weekend / End-of-Day Handover Protocol
This node does not currently exist in either the ES Visit or Post-Discharge workflow. It is the structural absence that produced Mr. Tan's situation. A promise made on Friday afternoon enters a 48-hour window with no named owner and no system record.
Joint Commission — named owner and contingency plan required before responsibility transfers at any handoff
Immediate actions — before CM leaves for the weekend
→ Step 1: CM logs open commitment in .cmutel with full detail: what was promised, to whom, by when, and what resolution requires
→ Step 2: CM attempts resolution before end of Friday business hours
Was the commitment resolved before end of Friday?
Yes — Resolved before weekend
CM calls patient to confirm
.cmutel updated: closed
End of tree for this commitment
No — Unresolved entering weekend
Is there identified clinical risk?
Yes — Risk identified
CM escalates to Consultant and Team Lead before leaving on Friday
Consultant determines whether weekend on-call team needs to hold this
On-call CM or duty officer is briefed: patient name, commitment details, what resolution requires, all contact numbers
Patient is called back Friday before close:
"We have not been able to reach [doctor/pharmacy] today. Someone from our team will follow up with you by [specific day and time]." — A named new commitment replaces the unresolved one
Owner: On-call / Duty Officer · Timeframe: Named day + time communicated to patient
No — No immediate clinical risk
CM flags in .cmutel: unresolved, low risk, handover required
CM writes handover note: open commitment, patient details, what is needed, suggested first action
Handover note placed in Team Lead's in-basket before Friday close
Team Lead assigns Monday morning owner before end of Friday
Owner: Named CM for Monday · Timeframe: First action by 10am Monday
4
Audit + detection
Patient Has Not Heard Back Within Promised Timeframe
In the current system, no one detects this unless the patient calls back. This node is the system catching what the current workflow cannot see. Broken promises generate no signal and are never counted.
OMH/DOH (2026) — failed attempts and unresolved needs must be documented and visible before disengagement
Detection mechanisms required
→ Open commitment entries in .cmutel reviewed at every subsequent patient contact
→ Team Lead conducts weekly open-commitment audit: any .cmutel entry flagged unresolved and older than 2 business days is escalated automatically
How long has the commitment been unresolved?
Less than 2 business days
Assign to CM as priority callback today
Owner: CM · Timeframe: Same business day
2–4 business days, no clinical risk
Team Lead assigns to CM or covering CM
Patient contacted to acknowledge delay and receive specific new timeframe
.cmutel updated with new commitment
Owner: Team Lead assigns · Timeframe: Contact patient within 24 hours
More than 4 business days — OR — clinical risk present at any point
Escalate to Consultant immediately
Consultant determines clinical response: is the medication gap now a safety issue?
Patient contacted by CM or Consultant directly
Incident logged for quality review
Owner: Consultant + Team Lead · Timeframe: Same business day, no exceptions
5
Unreachable patient path
Patient Cannot Be Reached When CM Attempts Resolution Callback
SAMHSA best effort practice — 3 attempts at different timings and days before escalation
Was the patient successfully reached?
Yes — Contact made
Resolution communicated to patient
.cmutel updated: commitment closed, outcome documented
End of tree for this commitment
No — First attempt, no contact
Document attempt in .cmutel with timestamp
Retry at a different time of day, same or next business day
Owner: CM · Timeframe: Within 1 business day of first attempt
No — Second attempt, still no contact
Document in .cmutel
Attempt contact with NOK or emergency contact if urgent need
Notify Team Lead: resolution pending, patient unreachable
Owner: CM + Team Lead · Timeframe: Within 2 business days of original promise
No — Third attempt, still no contact
Escalate to Consultant: patient was promised resolution, cannot be reached, need remains unresolved
Consultant determines whether home visit, on-call intervention, or GP notification is needed
All three attempts documented in .cmutel with timestamps and outcomes
Owner: Consultant · Timeframe: Decision within same business day as third failed attempt

Step 4 — Match tree to guidelines
How Each Step Reflects Best Practice
Tree Step Guideline It Reflects IMH Workflow Gap It Closes
Step 1
Log commitment before closing call		 Joint Commission Sentinel Event Alert 58 — every handoff requires a documented action list and contingency plan .cmutel has no open-commitment field. Call closes. Promise disappears. No audit trail exists.
Step 2
Same-day resolution with risk escalation		 SAMHSA (2019) — 24-hour follow-up is an institutional responsibility, not a personal one No same-day escalation path exists for CM commitments in either the ES Visit or Post-Discharge workflow.
Step 3
Weekend / EOD handover protocol		 Joint Commission — named owner and contingency plan required before responsibility transfers at any handoff No handover protocol exists for mid-call commitments in either workflow. The weekend gap is institutionally invisible.
Step 4
Open-commitment audit trigger		 OMH/DOH (2026) — failed attempts must be documented and surface-visible before disengagement Broken promises are never counted, never surfaced to supervisors, and generate no signal. The system cannot know they failed.
Step 5
3-attempt contact protocol		 SAMHSA best effort practice — 3 attempts at different timings and days before escalation is triggered Existing 3-attempt protocol applies to initial contact only. This tree extends it to resolution callbacks — closing a parallel gap.

The current IMH workflow ends when the contact is made. This decision tree begins where that workflow ends — at the moment an obligation is created inside a completed call. Every node addresses a gap the current workflow structurally cannot see. The CM on a heavy caseload Friday afternoon now has a logging step, a named owner for the weekend, a patient-facing communication protocol, and an escalation path that does not depend on memory alone. That is what the guidelines required all along. The tree is the first time it has been written down.
