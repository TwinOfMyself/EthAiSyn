What the Map Reveals
After-Hours Crisis Gap
The crisis escalation pathway assumes office hours. When a patient deteriorates at 10pm, the CM is working with a phone and a protocol that was not built for that moment.
  • Unclear after-hours pathways
  • Cannot authorize admission independently
  • Caregiver may be only contact — and may be in distress
Discharge Notification Delay
The CM's follow-up window opens when they receive the discharge notification — but the patient is already home. The most critical transition moment is often missed before the CM can act.
  • Notification arrives late or incomplete
  • 2-day follow-up clock starts too late
  • Patient already re-entering community unsupported
Community Partner Accountability
The CM coordinates community referrals and confirms acceptance — but has no authority to enforce partner follow-through. Partners operate on different systems. Information sharing is fragmented by design.
  • Referral waitlists outside CM control
  • No shared record system with external partners
  • Accountable for linkage, not for outcome
EHR as Institutional Accountability Tool
Documentation takes as long as the clinical interaction. The system was built to serve institutional compliance — not clinical time. The CM bears the burden of both.
  • Real-time updating impossible during high-volume periods
  • Inconsistent MDT standards create record gaps
  • Emotional load: documentation competes with care
Family / Caregiver as Information Relay
The caregiver is often the only reachable contact — but their role as an information source is informal, unstructured, and unverifiable. No defined responsibility exists for what they are expected to communicate, or when.
  • Contact numbers change without notification
  • Family distress distorts crisis reporting
  • No accountability structure for caregiver communication
The CM as Gap-Filler by Design
The map shows that most of the system's communication gaps and authority gaps resolve at one node: the case manager. That is not a coincidence. It is the function the role was designed to serve.
  • Every gap in Ring 2 routes through CM
  • Every authority gap in Ring 3 is CM's problem to navigate
  • Role exists because the system's gaps are permanent
