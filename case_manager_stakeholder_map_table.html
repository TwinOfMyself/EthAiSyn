Stakeholder Map

Case Manager · Scope of Work · April 2026

Every relationship in this map carries a different weight. Some are built on trust. Some on protocol. Some on the gap between what the system promised and what it can deliver. This table names them all.

Ring Stakeholder Key Responsibilities Daily Tasks & Workflows Pain Points & Frustrations Key Quotes
Role — Hat 01 & 03 Case Management
Ring 1
Direct Care		 Patient
  • Continuity & transition management
  • Risk surveillance & early intervention
  • Documentation & care-plan updates
  • Review chart at discharge notification
  • Telephonic follow-up within 2 working days
  • Monitor 1st TCU visit; meet patient in clinic
  • Conduct mental state & risk assessments
  • Document all contacts in hospital clinical system
  • Escalate deterioration to supervising clinician
  • OperationalDischarge notifications arrive late, compressing follow-up window
  • InformationSystem may not reflect real-time discharge status
  • AuthorityCannot compel attendance or override scheduling
  • ResourceHigh caseload limits monitoring capacity
 "Discharge is rarely clean. By the time I get the notification, the patient is already home and sometimes already in crisis." Paraphrase — qualitative study, community mental health CM
Ring 1
Direct Care		 Caregiver / Family
  • Family liaison & psychoeducation
  • Caregiver burden assessment
  • Crisis support & safety planning
  • Build rapport with caregiver at point of entry
  • Deliver psychoeducation on diagnosis & relapse signs
  • Assess caregiver stress and capacity
  • Provide crisis advice during acute episodes
  • Send TCU appointment reminders
  • EmotionalManaging family during crisis by phone is high-stakes and isolating
  • InformationCaregiver account during crisis may be incomplete or distressed
  • ResourceHome visit capacity limited by caseload
 "The family is often carrying as much as the patient. If I don't support them, the whole plan falls apart." Paraphrase — psychiatric CM, interview-based article
Ring 3
System		 EHR System
  • Documentation & care-plan updates
  • Clinical record management
  • Compliance & audit trail
  • Open and review case file at each interaction
  • Record all contacts, assessments & decisions
  • Update care plan after each MDT discussion
  • Cross-check notes against MDT meeting records
  • Flag incomplete documentation to supervising team
  • OperationalDocumentation takes as long as the clinical visit itself
  • InformationInconsistent MDT documentation creates record gaps
  • EmotionalPressure to document competes with direct care time
 "I'm accountable for what's in the record, but half the information I need isn't in the system when I need it." Paraphrase — psychiatric CM, qualitative research
Role — Hat 02 Care Coordination
Ring 2
Coordination		 MDT
  • Information integration across teams
  • Case formulation contribution
  • MDT meeting participation
  • Attend MDT meetings; contribute case summaries & risk updates
  • Consolidate assessment info from inpatient team & community
  • Clarify role boundaries to prevent duplication or gaps
  • Develop preliminary case formulation with MDT
  • InformationMDT members may hold conflicting views of patient status
  • AuthorityCan recommend but cannot enforce actions by MDT
  • OperationalPsychoeducation time-intensive across varying literacy levels
 "I'm the only person who talks to everyone. The left hand and the right hand don't always know what the other is doing." Paraphrase — care coordinator, community mental health
Ring 2
Coordination		 Inpatient Team
  • Continuity from inpatient to outpatient
  • Discharge planning coordination
  • Transition handoff management
  • Receive referral or discharge notification from inpatient team
  • Confirm discharge status and post-discharge needs
  • Coordinate outpatient TCU appointment scheduling
  • Ensure care plan reflects inpatient recommendations
  • InformationDischarge info arrives late or incomplete
  • OperationalInpatient and outpatient systems do not always communicate
  • AuthorityCannot expedite inpatient discharge decisions
 "I spend the first two days after discharge just trying to confirm someone is reachable." Paraphrase — behavioral health CM, professional forum
Ring 2
Coordination		 Outpatient / TCU Clinic
  • Follow-up appointment monitoring
  • Default management
  • TCU visit coordination
  • Monitor 1st TCU visit; meet patient in clinic
  • Confirm follow-up and address attendance barriers
  • Send TCU reminders prior to appointments
  • Manage default cases — follow up on missed appointments
  • ResourceClinic slots may not align with patient needs
  • OperationalManual reminder process is time-intensive
  • AuthorityCannot compel attendance; engagement is voluntary
 "Discharge is not a finish line. It's the start of a different kind of tracking." Paraphrase — case management practice literature
Ring 2
Coordination		 Community Partners
  • Community integration & referral
  • Psychoeducation delivery
  • Patient navigation support
  • Coordinate referrals; confirm community partner acceptance
  • Bridge patient between IMH, family, and community services
  • Monitor community partner follow-through
  • Provide psychoeducation to community partners
  • InformationPartners operate on different systems; sharing is fragmented
  • ResourceReferral waitlists delay coordination
  • AuthorityCannot enforce external partner actions
 "Psychoeducation works when there's time and trust. We don't always have either." Paraphrase — psychiatric social worker / CM hybrid role
Ring 2
Coordination		 Crisis Services
  • Crisis coordination & escalation
  • De-escalation & safety planning
  • Early intervention formulation
  • Monitor pre-crisis indicators through scheduled & unscheduled contacts
  • Attempt telephonic de-escalation when distress reported
  • Activate crisis protocol; notify supervising clinician
  • Formulate safety plan with patient & caregiver
  • Document crisis episode; debrief with MDT post-resolution
  • OperationalCrisis calls arrive outside scheduled hours; after-hours pathways unclear
  • ResourceEmergency beds are scarce; escalation doesn't guarantee placement
  • AuthorityCannot independently authorize inpatient admission
  • EmotionalManaging acute distress by phone is high-stakes and isolating
 "Crisis doesn't wait for appointments. By the time the next visit is scheduled, the situation has already escalated." Paraphrase — mental health CM, qualitative study
Role — Hat 01 Therapeutic Role
Ring 1
Direct Care		 Patient (Therapeutic)
  • Therapeutic engagement & alliance
  • Counselling & supportive therapy
  • Home visit delivery
  • Establish initial rapport at point of entry into care coordination
  • Provide individual counselling and supportive therapy
  • Conduct home visits when patient unable or unwilling to attend clinic
  • Adjust therapeutic approach based on changing mental state
  • EmotionalBuilding alliance with resistant patients is slow and demanding
  • ResourceHome visit capacity limited by geographic spread and caseload
  • AuthorityCannot compel engagement — relationship is voluntary
 "You can't rush trust. Especially with patients who've been let down by the system before." Paraphrase — mental health CM, professional association profile
Role — Hat 03 System Navigation
Ring 3
System		 Supervisor / Clinical Lead
  • Upward accountability
  • Escalation & consultation
  • Quality oversight reporting
  • Escalate high-risk cases to supervising clinician
  • Seek consultation on complex case formulations
  • Report on caseload, risk flags, and unresolved gaps
  • Participate in supervision sessions and performance review
  • OperationalSupervision timing does not align with real-time crisis needs
  • AuthorityKey decisions require supervisor authorization
  • EmotionalCarrying complex high-risk caseloads with insufficient support
 "I can see it coming. The system doesn't always give me the tools to stop it." Paraphrase — psychiatric CM interview
Ring 3
System		 Referring Physician / IMH
  • Referral intake & case entry
  • Clinical handoff coordination
  • Institutional liaison
  • Receive and review referral from physician or IMH team
  • Confirm case eligibility and assign to care pathway
  • Communicate care plan updates back to referring physician
  • Coordinate actions between IMH and outpatient settings
  • InformationReferral documentation often incomplete on receipt
  • OperationalPhysicians may not be reachable for timely consultation
  • AuthorityCannot act on referral gaps without physician sign-off
 "We're responsible for the transition, but we don't control the resources." Paraphrase — behavioral health CM, IHI care transitions literature
Ring 3
System		 Policy, Regulation & Quality
  • Regulatory compliance
  • Standards of practice adherence
  • Quality & safety reporting
  • Comply with mandated timelines (e.g., 2-day post-discharge follow-up)
  • Follow CMSA standards of practice for formulation and documentation
  • Participate in quality audits and safety reviews when required
  • OperationalCompliance requirements generate administrative overhead
  • InformationPolicy updates not always communicated clearly to frontline staff
  • AuthorityRegulatory constraints limit clinical flexibility in complex cases
