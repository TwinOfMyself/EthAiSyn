Ishikawa Diagram · RCA

Why do verbal callback commitments
go unresolved?

Problem
Statement
Case Managers make verbal callback commitments to patients that are not tracked or followed up — resulting in unresolved urgent needs, particularly when calls occur near end-of-day or before weekends.
Cause Categories — Detailed Breakdown
People
CM caseload is too high to follow through Commitment made in good faith; capacity prevents execution
No designated backup for open patient tasks Promise lives in one person's memory with no redundancy
Process ↔ Why 3 & 4
No step in the workflow to log open commitments Making a promise is a complete action — resolution is not required
No end-of-shift handover protocol for unresolved items Open loops are invisible when the CM clocks off

★ Root Cause

The system has no mechanism to record, track, or escalate verbal commitments made by Case Managers to patients. When a CM promises a callback, that commitment exists only in their memory — with no log, no alert, no handover, and no accountability structure. When competing urgencies arise (as they routinely do on a Friday afternoon), the promise falls silently with no one catching it. The failure is not individual negligence; it is a predictable outcome of a workflow that never designed for promise accountability. One unmet callback at a critical juncture becomes the moment a patient concludes the system cannot be trusted.

5 Whys — Questions to Ask in a Stakeholder Conversation
1
Why are verbal callback commitments not being followed up?
"Can you walk me through what happens after a Case Manager tells a patient they'll call them back — is there any step where that gets recorded anywhere?"
2
Why aren't callback commitments recorded in the system?
"Is there a designated place in the EMR or any shared tool where Case Managers are expected to log patient promises or pending action items?"
3
Why is there no structured place to log pending commitments?
"How does the team currently track tasks that are unresolved at the end of a shift — is there a handover process, or does each Case Manager manage their own to-do list independently?"
4
Why doesn't a handover process exist for unresolved patient commitments?
"When a Case Manager can't reach a prescribing doctor or resolve an issue before end-of-day, what is the expected protocol — is there guidance on what to do with that open loop?"
5
Why is there no protocol for open loops at end-of-day or before weekends?
"Has the team ever formally reviewed what happens to urgent patient needs that come in on Friday afternoons — and is there anyone responsible for ensuring those don't fall through the weekend gap?"
